About

troveTaste is an experiment about digital archives overview. WHen you visit for the first time a digital archive it use to be a lack of explanations about what actually the archive contents are. TroveTaste is a very small effort in this direction: iproposes some adies on giving you a taste of an archive. The more you search, the more you will see what the archive contents are about.

It uses the Trove API, the API of the big archive of the National Library of Australia.

It contents almost 250 Million items, grouped in 9 categories:

  • pictures: 4903410
  • maps: 389341
  • books: 17428770
  • musics: 2553743
  • peoples: 913785
  • newspapers: 78533033
  • articles: 142458865
  • lists: 19429
  • collections: 53215
  • Trove Total Records: 247732527

troveTaste is developed by Jaume Nualart as part of the PhD in Communication in the University of Canberra (Des2012-Jan2013).

How it works? WHat it does?

You can search for works (It uses an English-stop-words list, to avoid empty words). TroveTaste will give you 2 main outputs:

  1. A semi-cercles representation showing the relative/absolute importante of the query in the archive.
