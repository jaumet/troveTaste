About

troveTaste is an experiment about digital archives overview. When you visit a digital archive for the first time it uses to have a lack of explanations about what actually the archive contents are. TroveTaste is a very small effort in solving this situation. It gives you a taste of the archive, the more you search, the more you see what the archive contents are about.

It uses the great Trove API, the API of the big archive of the National Library of Australia.

It contents more than 300 Million items.The main categories the following nine:

  • pictures: 7312374
  • maps: 523351
  • books: 17455470
  • musics: 2664292
  • people: 914577
  • newspapers: 80392994
  • articles: 143516886
  • lists: 31711
  • collections: 532151
  • Trove Total Records: 324344265
[static data taken on Des 2012]

troveTaste is developed by Jaume Nualart as part of the PhD in Communication in the University of Canberra (Des2012-Jan2013).

How it works? What it does?

You can search for words (it uses an English-stop-words list to avoid empty words). Everytime someone searches in troveTaste, the query and the results are added to the database and a table shows a comparation of the current query with the ones stored in the database. TroveTaste gives you two outputs:

  1. A semi-cercles representation showing the relative/absolute importante of the query in the archive.
